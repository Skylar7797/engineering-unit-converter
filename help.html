Help & Support

Q1: How do I use the Unit Converter?

To use the Unit Converter, first enter the numeric value you want to convert into the input field. Next, select the unit you are converting from and the unit you are converting to using the dropdown menus. Once your selections are made, click the Convert button. The result will display below, showing the converted value in the selected unit.

Note that some conversions may require attention to decimal precision. For example, converting large lengths or tiny masses can result in values with many decimal places. The converter is designed to handle a wide range of units accurately for engineering purposes.

Q2: How do I use the Scientific Calculator?

The Scientific Calculator allows you to perform both basic arithmetic and advanced functions such as trigonometry, logarithms, and exponents. Enter numbers using the on-screen buttons or your keyboard. Use the function keys for operations like sin, cos, tan, log, and ln. Press the equal button "=" to get the result.

Be aware of the angle mode indicator at the top-left of the display. You can toggle between DEG (degrees) and RAD (radians) to match your calculations. Using the correct angle mode is essential for accurate trigonometric results.

Q3: How do I take notes in the Notes panel?

The Notes panel on the right-hand side allows you to jot down important calculations, reminders, or unit conversions. Click inside the text area and type freely. You can resize the box vertically to fit more content. This panel is designed to store temporary notes for your current session.

Remember that notes are not automatically saved to your device. You can copy content if you want to keep it for later reference. The Notes panel is intended to complement the calculator and converter for an efficient workflow.

Q4: Can I convert between any two units?

The converter supports a wide range of engineering units, including length, mass, temperature, and more. You can convert between most standard units within each category. However, conversions between unrelated categories (e.g., length to mass) are not supported.

Ensure that the "from" and "to" units are in the same category to get accurate results. If a unit is missing or unavailable, it may be outside the current supported set, and you can suggest additional units for future updates.

Q5: What should I do if a calculation seems incorrect?

If a result appears incorrect, double-check the input values and the selected units or functions. Misaligned units or incorrect angle modes can lead to unexpected outcomes. Always verify your selections before interpreting the result.

Additionally, check that you are using the proper function buttons and that no unintended keys were pressed. The calculator is designed for accuracy, but human error in input is the most common source of incorrect calculations.

Q6: Can I use the site on mobile devices?

Yes, the Engineering Workbench is fully responsive and can be accessed on smartphones and tablets. All calculator and converter functions adapt to smaller screens, allowing you to perform calculations on the go. Navigation menus collapse appropriately to optimize space, and input fields remain user-friendly.

For best performance, use the latest mobile browser versions. Some very old browsers may have limitations in rendering the grid layouts or JavaScript-based interactive elements.
