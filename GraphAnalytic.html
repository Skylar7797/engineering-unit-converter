Graph Analytic

Graph Analytic in Engineering Workbench is a comprehensive real-time graph visualization and analysis platform tailored for engineers, students, and data enthusiasts. It enables dynamic plotting of a wide range of mathematical functions, including trigonometric (sin, cos, tan), exponential, logarithmic, polynomial, and composite functions. Users can explore function behaviors with flexible X and Y axis scales in linear or logarithmic modes, providing both detailed local insights and broad-range analysis. The tool supports multiple cursor modes: a function-following cursor for precise slope and derivative evaluation, and a free cursor for general coordinate inspection. Users can plot up to three functions simultaneously, compare them visually, and instantly analyze zero-crossings, extrema, and slope trends. Floating info boxes provide real-time numerical feedback, including coordinates, derivatives, and unit conversions between radians and degrees.

Optimized for high-resolution rendering and smooth updates, Graph Analytic ensures accurate display even for complex functions. Preset engineering and mathematical functions allow quick exploration, while user-defined inputs enable complete customization. The tool’s interface is designed for clarity, interactivity, and efficiency, making it suitable for educational purposes, engineering design, research, and data analysis. Advanced features such as automatic extrema detection, integration and differentiation visualization, and interactive zooming and panning are under active development. Future updates will continue to expand functionality, improve usability, and provide a more robust experience for precise and intuitive function analysis.

By combining real-time plotting, interactive analysis, and clear visualization, Graph Analytic empowers users to explore, analyze, and understand mathematical functions thoroughly. Our ongoing commitment is to enhance the platform with advanced analytical tools, performance optimizations, and user-friendly improvements, ensuring that engineers, researchers, and students can rely on Graph Analytic as a powerful, accurate, and versatile component of the Engineering Workbench suite.
